# Key Sources for Paper 1

This file records citation anchors for the MAPS empirical paper. It separates
general theory, directly relevant longitudinal MNLFA work, and MAPS-specific
contextual evidence.

## Longitudinal Measurement Invariance and Growth

Widaman, Ferrer, and Conger (2010) provide the basic longitudinal SEM argument:
researchers need evidence that repeated indicators measure the same construct on
a common metric before interpreting factor mean or growth differences as change.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1750-8606.2009.00110.x
- PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20369028/

Liu et al. (2017) extend longitudinal measurement-invariance logic to
ordered-categorical indicators, which is directly relevant because the MAPS
acculturative-stress responses are ordinal Likert-type items.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1037/met0000075

Kim and Willson (2014) show via simulation that measurement noninvariance can
bias latent growth parameters and inflate inferential errors in growth modeling.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/10705511.2014.915374
- Source page: https://digitalcommons.usf.edu/edq_facpub/1/

## MNLFA and Longitudinal MNLFA

Bauer (2017) positions MNLFA as a general framework for testing measurement
invariance and DIF across multiple categorical or continuous moderators.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1037/met0000077

Bauer, Belzak, and Cole (2020) extend the MNLFA discussion to settings with
multiple background covariates and regularized DIF detection.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/10705511.2019.1642754
- PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33132679/

Chen and Bauer (2024) are the central methodological anchor for this paper.
They argue that conventional growth models can confound construct change with
changes in measurement and propose a longitudinal moderated factor analysis
approach for modeling both.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1037/met0000685
- PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39207378/

Chen and Bauer (2026) is a very direct new comparison paper: it contrasts
longitudinal MNLFA with conventional first-order growth models and emphasizes
that mean/sum-score growth models do not account for DIF-related measurement
change.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/00273171.2026.2640576
- Source page: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00273171.2026.2640576

## Response Shift and Measurement Bias

King-Kallimanis, Oort, and Garst (2010) treat response shift as a special case
of measurement bias in longitudinal data and describe how SEM can be used to
separate common-factor change from changes in the measurement model.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s10182-010-0129-y

## MAPS-Specific Contextual Evidence

Lee and Choi (2026) used MAPS 2022 data, a graded response model, and a MIMIC
approach to validate a related acculturative-stress scale for mothers from
multicultural families. They reported discrimination-related DIF in 5 of 7
items.

Use this as contextual support, not direct replication, because the item set,
wave structure, and model differ from the present longitudinal MNLFA.

- KCI: https://www.kci.go.kr/kciportal/ci/sereArticleSearch/ciSereArtiView.kci?sereArticleSearchBean.artiId=ART003314865
- Citation: Lee, C., & Choi, Y. (2026). Psychometric Validation of the Cultural
  Adaptation Stress Scale for Mothers from Multicultural Families Using the
  Graded Response Model. Korean Journal of Convergence Science, 15(2), 379-395.
